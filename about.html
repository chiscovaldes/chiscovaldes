About

Chisco Valdés is a filmmaker and photographer, born in Guatemala based in Spain. His interests revolve around issues of identity, religion, dualities, and daily contradictions. Using the image as a means of investigating, his photographic work uses the mixture of fiction and reality in different formats to explore ambiguities and contradictions in cultural documentation of daily life.

Graduated for BA in Communications in Spain, as a student, produced, co-wrote, shot the short films Verte and Eclipse selected in more than 30 festivals worldwide and won 9 awards. He has worked in Spain, UK, USA, and Guatemala commercials, music videos, fiction, and documentaries in various roles, with proven skills in direction and cinematography. Selected for the residence 20 Fotógrafos sponsored by NatGeo in 2019 and for the Iberoamerican Meeting of Emerging Filmmakers Ibermedia 2019. His photographic work has been exhibited, featured, and published in Spain, Guatemala, Italy, and the USA, in media such as GuatePhoto, Festival Casa América, C41 among others.

He is currently in the development of his project Janydman, in which he documents his experience of becoming a handyman for 8 months in the United States.
